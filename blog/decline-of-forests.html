How many trees are there?

Based on a 2015 global census, half the trees have perished on Earth

Posted by Harry on August 2, 2016

There are an estimated three trillion trees on Earth. Roughly 420 trees per person. It may seem like a lot but it is a statement of how many trees there are. Each year, 15 billion trees are either cut down or burned down. Predictions indicate that in 40 years time, the trees will be gone.

Trees help absorb CO2 emissions; less trees means that more CO2 gases are in the atmosphere. Unfortunately, we've contributed to the decline. We've reduced around 46 percent of the trees, that's almost half the number of trees on the planet. Trees are cut down for agriculture and construction supplies. As our population increase, we need more space and food.

Climate change will not be the only consequence. The well-being of all living beings on this planet will be jeopardized.

So, what can we do?

First, we need to acknowledge that the decline of trees is an important issue to discuss in our communitities. Second, we must understand what is contributing to the loss of these trees and how we can lobby against those actions. Three, we need to understand how important trees are to our environment and our lives.
